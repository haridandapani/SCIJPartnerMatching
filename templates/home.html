Step 1: On Google Forms, download student responses from the "SCIJ Case Pairings Form" as a CSV or (preferably) an Excel file. To get an idea of what this file should look like, you may click on "Download Example Data File" to download an example. Upload your file below:




Step 2: Using Google Sheets or Excel, create a data spreadsheet that contains the headers and types of headers that should be taken into account when we generate pairings. This spreadsheet should contain three columns with the words "header," "type," and "necessary" in the first row of each column. The headers should match the headers in the Case Pairings Form spreadsheet you downloaded in Step 1. The "necessary" column should consist of 1s and 0s. To get an idea of what this file should look like, you may click on "Download Example Headers File" to download an example. Once you have finalized your spreadsheet, upload the file below:




Step 3: Choose the minimum number of hours that two students must share in order to be a legal match.
